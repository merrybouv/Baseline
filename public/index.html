Before you continue

This is a research demonstration for adults. It shows, side by side, how a general AI response differs from one conditioned on a declared developmental stage. It is not a product, and it is not for children to use.

By continuing you confirm you are 18 or older and understand this is an illustrative demonstration of why AI products and EdTech used by people under 18 need a framework like this.

No prompts are stored. This demonstration does not log or retain what you type.

Baseline · working title

A developmental framework for AI responses to people under 18

Type a prompt. The left shows a general AI response. The right shows the same prompt conditioned on a declared developmental band, with the framework's decisions made visible. Band is declared, never inferred.

Literature this framework is grounded in

Developmental capacity

  • ICO Age Appropriate Design Code, Annex B (age/developmental stages).
  • Common Sense Media, per-age content guidance.
  • Piaget, J. (1952). Concrete vs. formal operational stages.
  • American Academy of Pediatrics, media guidance.

Theory of mind & anthropomorphism

  • Kurian, N. (2025). Developmentally aligned AI. AI, Brain and Child.
  • Kurian, N. (2025). Relational drift and developmental vulnerability (preprint).
  • Goldman et al. (2025); Hoffman et al. (2021); attachment work (Bowlby; Ainsworth).

Content-harm gating

  • Ofcom, Protection of Children Codes / Online Safety Act — primary-priority content.
  • COPPA — the under-13 line and the 13–17 "teen gap."

Reading level / complexity

  • Flesch-Kincaid grade level; Lexile; Common Core text-complexity bands.

Routing / talking about harm

  • NCMEC / NetSmartz; NSPCC; Thorn; Australia's eSafety Commissioner.
